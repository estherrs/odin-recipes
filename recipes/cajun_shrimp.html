Cajun Shrimp Boil

Description

This is an easy and tasty classic shrimp boil with baby red potatoes, corn on the cob, and Andouille sausages generously seasoned with Creole Seasoning and garnished with lemon for a fresh and vibrant summer outdoor meal ready in less than an hour.

Ingredients

  • salt
  • 2 small sprigs of thyme
  • 1 pound of baby red potatoes cut into small chunks
  • 4 ears of corn cut into chunks
  • 1/3 cup of butter
  • 1 tbsp of Creole Seasoning
  • 2 tsp of minced garlic
  • 1 tsp of thyme
  • 1 pound of shrimp, peeled
  • 12 ounce of Andouille Sausage
  • cilantro, chopped

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F. Spray a sheet pan with a nonstick spray and set aside.
  2. Fill a large pot with water, season with salt, and then add the potatoes, and thyme sprigs. Cover and bring to a boil, then reduce to a simmer and cook about 8-10 minutes or until tender. Potatoes should be firm.
  3. Add corn and cook for 3-5 minutes. Drain water.
  4. In a small pan, combine the butter, creole seasoning, garlic, and thyme.
  5. On medium-low heat, saute them for 30-60 seconds and remove them from heat.
  6. Add the Creole butter sauce to the shrimp, potatoes, and corn and give it a stir until everything is evenly coated.
  7. Line all the ingredients in a single layer on a baking sheet and add the Andouille sausages. Sprinkle with salt, pepper, and Creole Seasoning.
  8. Bake for about 12-15 minutes, or until shrimp is pink and cooked through.
  9. Serve garnished with chopped cilantro.
